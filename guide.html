Using Piano MIDI Viewer in Online Lessons

Option 1: Share your window

The app you use for video calls most likely has everything you need built in

Most video call apps let you share a specific window from your computer. When you share the Piano MIDI Viewer window, your student sees the piano keyboard as the main view, and your webcam stays on as a smaller picture.

The general steps are the same in every app:

  1. Start your video call as usual
  2. Look for a Share Screen button — it usually looks like a monitor with an arrow, and might also be called "Present" or "Screencast"
  3. Pick the Piano MIDI Viewer window from the list
  4. Your student now sees the piano — and your face stays visible too

If your app isn't listed below, these steps will most likely still work — the button names and layout may look a little different, but the idea is the same. If your app doesn't support window sharing at all, scroll down to Option 2.

Here is how it looks in some of the most popular apps. Keep in mind that the design of these apps changes over time, but the principle stays the same.

Zoom webcam + piano
  1. During a call, click the green Share Screen button at the bottom
  2. A window appears showing all your open apps — click on Piano MIDI Viewer
  3. Click the blue Share button
  4. Your student now sees the piano as the main view, with your webcam in the corner
  5. To stop sharing, click the red Stop Share button at the top of your screen

On macOS, Zoom may ask for screen recording permission the first time. Go to System Settings → Privacy & Security → Screen Recording and allow Zoom.

Google Meet webcam + piano
  1. During a call, click Present now at the bottom (monitor icon with an arrow)
  2. Choose A window
  3. Pick Piano MIDI Viewer from the list and click Share
  4. Your student now sees the piano as the main view, with your webcam as a smaller tile
  5. To stop, click the Stop sharing button

On macOS, your browser may need screen recording permission. Go to System Settings → Privacy & Security → Screen Recording and allow your browser (Chrome, Firefox, etc.).

Discord webcam + piano
  1. Join a voice channel or start a call
  2. Click Share Your Screen at the bottom (monitor icon)
  3. Switch to the Applications tab
  4. Pick Piano MIDI Viewer and click Go Live
  5. Turn on your camera separately (camera button) — both streams work at the same time

Free Discord accounts are limited to 720p 30fps screen sharing. Higher quality requires Discord Nitro.

Telegram Desktop webcam + piano
  1. During a video call, click the screencast button (monitor icon) in the call controls
  2. Pick the Piano MIDI Viewer window and click Share
  3. Your webcam stays on — your student sees both the piano and your face

On macOS, Telegram may need screen recording permission. Go to System Settings → Privacy & Security → Screen Recording and allow Telegram.

Option 2: OBS Virtual Camera

A powerful free tool that works with any video call app — takes a bit of setup, but only once

OBS is a free, open source app used by millions of streamers and content creators. It can combine your webcam and the piano into one video feed, then send it to any video call app as a "virtual camera".

The setup takes about 5 minutes. After that, you just open OBS before your lesson and click one button.

OBS is also great for creating pre-recorded lessons and YouTube videos — the same setup works for recording and streaming, not just live calls.

1 Download OBS

Go to obsproject.com and download OBS Studio for your system. It's free, open source, and used by millions of people worldwide.

Install it like any other app. When it opens for the first time, you can skip the auto-configuration wizard — we'll set everything up manually.

2 Get to know the interface

OBS may look complex, but you only need to know four things:

  • Main preview — the big area in the center. This is what your student will see.
  • Scenes — bottom left. Think of these as saved layouts you can switch between.
  • Sources — right next to Scenes. These are the things in your layout (webcam, piano app, etc.).
  • Controls — bottom right. This is where you'll start the Virtual Camera.

3 Create a scene

In the Scenes panel (bottom left), click the + button to add a new scene. Name it something like "Online Lesson".

This scene will hold your webcam and the piano app together. You can create other scenes later if you want different layouts.

4 Add your webcam

In the Sources panel, click the + button and choose Video Capture Device.

Give it a name (like "Webcam"), click OK, then pick your camera from the dropdown. You should see yourself in the main preview. Click OK to confirm.

5 Add Piano MIDI Viewer

Click the + button in Sources again and choose Window Capture.

Give it a name (like "Piano"), click OK, then pick Piano MIDI Viewer from the window list. You should see the piano keyboard appear in the preview. Click OK.

Make sure Piano MIDI Viewer is running before this step — otherwise it won't show up in the list.

6 Arrange the layout

Now you have both sources in the preview. Drag and resize them to create the layout you want:

  • Piano on top — put the piano keyboard across the top or bottom, with your webcam filling the rest
  • Side by side — webcam on one side, piano on the other
  • Picture-in-picture — piano small in a corner over your webcam

Drag the red handles to resize. Click and drag to move. The source that's higher in the Sources list appears on top.

You can use the eye icon next to each source to temporarily hide it — useful for showing just your webcam during parts of the lesson.

7 Start Virtual Camera

In the Controls panel (bottom right), click Start Virtual Camera.

This creates a virtual webcam on your computer that shows whatever's in the OBS preview. Any video call app will see it as a regular camera.

8 Use it in your video call

Open your video call app (Zoom, Meet, Discord, Telegram — anything). In the camera/video settings, pick OBS Virtual Camera instead of your regular webcam.

Your student now sees the combined view — your face and the piano keyboard, exactly as it looks in OBS.

For your next lesson: Just open OBS, make sure the "Online Lesson" scene is selected, click Start Virtual Camera, and choose OBS Virtual Camera in your call app. It takes about 10 seconds once you've done the initial setup.
