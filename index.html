Made for online lessons and video content

Piano MIDI Viewer shows what you play on a clean, simple keyboard on your screen. Share it in a video call so your student can follow along, or put it in a video for your viewers.

It's not a piano library, not a sampler, not a synth. It's a visual tool that makes piano teaching and learning easier.

Animation: video call with webcam + Piano MIDI Viewer side by side
Animation: USB cable plugs in → app detects piano → keys light up

Easy to set up

Connect your digital piano to your computer with a USB cable. No drivers needed — it just works. The app finds your piano automatically.

Most video call apps can already share the piano window alongside your webcam. No extra software needed. Our setup guide walks you through it step by step.

Mark keys and save screenshots

Use the pencil tool to mark any key — scales, chords, finger positions. Save a screenshot with one click. Build a library of cards for your students, or use it live during a lesson to explain something on the spot.

Ask your student to mark the right notes as a quick test. Clear, colorful images that look great even on a phone screen — perfect for short-form video content.

Animation: pencil marks a scale → screenshot saved → image in chat

Made by a musician, for musicians

This is a solo passion project with no budget and no company behind it. I'm a piano teacher, not a developer — I built this because I needed it and nothing like it existed.

There are no tricks, no ads, no tracking, no account needed. The app works completely offline. I believe education should be a right, not a privilege — so this will always be free.
